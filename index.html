01 / Diagnosis

Spending on growth, still unable to say what creates pipeline.

Paid is running. Content is publishing. SEO is producing traffic. Email sequences are live. But the channels are not connected to each other, attribution stops at last click, and CRM source data is incomplete on a meaningful portion of pipeline.

The result: budget decisions are made on last-click data that over-credits the final touch and hides the channels that created the opportunity. CAC climbs. The explanation stays unclear. More budget gets poured into a system that is already fragmented.

Worked example

A prospect finds you through LinkedIn, reads three posts, sees a retargeting ad, attends a webinar, then searches your brand name and requests a demo. Last click credits brand search. LinkedIn, content, retargeting, and nurture disappear from the decision. Budget follows the wrong signal.

06 / A Sample Finding

Illustrative example. Not a real client result.

Paid search is producing trials, but 60% of spend is going to low-intent segments that rarely convert to pipeline.

The issue is not volume. Clicks and trials are coming in. The issue is conversion quality, routing, and follow-up. High-intent searches (branded, competitor, problem-aware) are converting at 3x the rate of broad and informational traffic, but the budget is spread roughly evenly across all of them. Trial-to-pipeline conversion is also weak: no urgency sequence, no intent-based routing to sales, and no visibility into which trials came from which campaigns. The fix is not more spend. It is restructuring the campaign, tightening the trial routing, and connecting trial source data to the CRM so pipeline attribution becomes visible.

This is the kind of disconnect a revenue system audit is designed to surface: not a general recommendation, but a specific disconnect with a specific fix order.
