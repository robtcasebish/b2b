The Problem

Most B2B SaaS revenue problems are system problems, not channel problems.

Adding more budget to a disconnected system does not fix the system. If paid, SEO, content, and email are not working together, and attribution is broken, you cannot tell what is driving pipeline and what is wasting spend.

Revenue-stage B2B SaaS companies rarely have a traffic problem. They have a conversion and attribution problem: spend flowing in, pipeline not connecting back to the channels that drove it, CAC climbing without a clear explanation.

Common finding: 40 to 60 percent of a typical B2B SaaS paid budget is allocated based on last-click attribution that misrepresents which channels are actually contributing to pipeline. Budget decisions are made on the wrong signal.

Fragmented system

What most B2B SaaS revenue systems look like

  • Paid, SEO, content, and email running independently with no shared signal
  • Attribution stops at last click, so multi-touch channel contribution is invisible
  • Landing pages generic, not matched to ICP, segment, or buying intent
  • Pipeline source unknown in CRM for a significant portion of deals
  • CAC climbing without a clear understanding of which channels are responsible
  • No structured weekly review, so optimization is reactive and ad hoc

Connected system

What the same stack looks like when the system works

  • Paid, SEO, content, and email coordinated around shared ICP segments and buying signals
  • Multi-touch attribution shows which channels and content are contributing to pipeline
  • Landing pages matched to segment, channel, and intent: trial, demo, or content by stage
  • Every deal in the CRM has a source path connected to the channels that influenced it
  • CAC tracked by channel, by segment, by quarter: budget decisions are data-driven
  • Weekly review cadence: what changed, what to test, what to scale or cut
